Predictive Modeling of Tailpipe CO₂ Emissions for Early Vehicle Design

Conducted a statistical study to predict vehicle tailpipe CO₂ emissions at the early design stage. Used regression-based modeling to evaluate the influence of engine size, transmission type, fuel economy (MPG), and vehicle category on emissions and petroleum consumption.

Excel Statistical Modeling Regression Analysis Hypothesis Testing Data Visualization
Key Results/Insights:
  • Fuel economy and engine size were the most significant predictors of emissions.
  • Regression analysis confirmed strong correlations between vehicle design variables and annual petroleum consumption.
  • Results provide insights to guide early vehicle design decisions toward lower emissions.
House Price Prediction

Built predictive models to estimate housing prices based on features such as location, size, number of rooms, and amenities. Applied multiple regression and ensemble learning methods to improve prediction accuracy.

Python Pandas NumPy Scikit-learn Matplotlib Seaborn
Key Results/Insights:
  • Random Forest and XGBoost models achieved the best predictive accuracy compared to baseline linear regression.
  • Feature importance analysis showed square footage, location, and view as the strongest predictors.
  • Results demonstrate the effectiveness of ensemble methods for real estate price forecasting.
